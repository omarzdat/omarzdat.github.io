Select Committee into Stillbirth in South Australia

The extent and impact of stillbirth in South Australia is the focus of a significant new inquiry aimed at reducing loss and better supporting women and families.

And you can now have your say!

Still Aware plays a crucial role in raising awareness and providing education about stillbirth prevention—a vital yet often overlooked issue in public health. Through our dedicated efforts, we empower expectant parents with life-saving information about safe pregnancy practices, while advocating for improved healthcare policies and services.

By focusing on both prevention and support, Still Aware not only helps reduce the incidence of stillbirth but also provides critical emotional and practical support for families navigating this incredibly difficult experience.

Our work has contributed significantly to increasing awareness, improving clinical practices, and creating a more informed, compassionate response to stillbirth—ultimately saving lives and fostering a supportive community for families.

The SA Select Committee Inquiry

Ms Savvas has already met with medical experts in this field from the Women's and Children's Hospital, as well as community support groups, to hear about the work that is already underway in this space, and how we could assist further.

The Committee Will Investigate and Report On:

Best practice stillbirth prevention measures for expectant parents
Public education programs to increase community awareness of stillbirth and where people can access support
Models of care in pregnancy and after stillbirth, including for priority populations
Follow-up care including mental health support or care in subsequent pregnancies for parents after stillbirth
Support and training for healthcare professionals
Data collection, reporting and monitoring
Allocation of research efforts

This inquiry represents a critical opportunity to drive systemic change in how stillbirth is understood, prevented, and responded to in South Australia.

The outcomes of this committee have the potential to save lives, improve care for families experiencing loss, and ensure that healthcare professionals have the training and resources they need to provide the best possible support.
