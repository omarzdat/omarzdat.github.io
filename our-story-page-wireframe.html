Our Story

Australia's first Stillbirth Awareness Charity

In Australia, Stillbirth is the leading cause of infant death. Many of these deaths are preventable. Still Aware's mission is to end preventable stillbirth through awareness and education.

Still Aware was founded in 2014 by Claire Foord-Heath, after Alfie Foord-Heath was born without breath on February 12, 2014. With no rhyme or reason for the death of this healthy baby girl, her mother and father - under the guidance of the Still Aware Board and Clinical Advisory Board, along with your help - seek to change how stillbirth is understood prior to the tragedy, educating for awareness and raising awareness for prevention.

Our Team

[Claire Photo]
Claire

Our Founder, Claire Foord

Claire founded Still Aware in 2014 after the stillbirth of her daughter Alfie. Determined to prevent other families from experiencing this tragedy, Claire has dedicated herself to raising awareness and advocating for change in pregnancy health. Her efforts have been recognized with South Australian of the Year in 2018. Claire's leadership continues to drive Still Aware's mission forward.
