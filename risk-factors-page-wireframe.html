Understanding Risk Factors

Are you a clinician? Access our published research data to help identify risk factors for stillbirth. Through antenatal care, each expectant family's individual history should be considered, with open conversations about potential risk factors.

Important Disclaimer
This information is not intended to replace the advice of a trained medical professional. Still Aware provides this knowledge as a courtesy, not as a substitute for personalized medical advice. We encourage expectant families and clinicians to have informed conversations about risk factors. Quality antenatal care that is accessible to all has the potential to reduce stillbirth rates. Multiple risk factors may warrant closer or more regular monitoring, particularly in the third trimester.

Key Research Findings

Implementation of national perinatal mortality audit programs aimed at improving the quality of care could substantially reduce stillbirths.

Microarray analysis is more likely than karyotype analysis to provide a genetic diagnosis, primarily because of its success with nonviable tissue. This is especially valuable in analyses of stillbirths with congenital anomalies or in cases where karyotype results cannot be obtained.
