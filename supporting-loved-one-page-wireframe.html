Supporting a Loved One

Guidance for family, friends and colleagues navigating how to support someone after pregnancy loss.

When someone you care about experiences stillbirth, knowing what to say or do can feel overwhelming—especially when you're processing your own grief for the baby you've lost as a grandparent, sibling, friend, or colleague.

"

While it may be terrifying to imagine saying or doing the wrong thing, it's important to know that saying nothing is worst.

Understanding what your loved one is experiencing is a powerful first step. You're not here to fix their pain—that's not possible. But by learning about their journey, you'll feel more equipped to offer meaningful support.

Grief looks different for everyone. Rather than making assumptions about what they need, the simplest and most powerful question you can ask is: "How can I help?"

Your presence and support can profoundly impact how bereaved parents navigate their grief. Even small gestures of care can make an enormous difference.
